Havana, Cuba

Why is Havana Off-Beat?

Havana is more than just a Caribbean beach destination. It's a vibrant city with history, culture, and an undeniable charm. Here's why it's off-beat:

  • Unique Culture: A fascinating blend of Spanish, African, and American influences.
  • Time Capsule Vibe: Classic architecture, vintage cars, and a slower pace of life create a unique atmosphere.
  • Authentic Experiences: Engage with locals, explore hidden gems, and savor genuine Cuban hospitality.

Getting to Havana

By Air:

The most common way to reach Havana is by flying into José Martí International Airport (HAV). Numerous airlines offer direct flights from North America, Europe, and other parts of the world.

By Cruise Ship:

Cuba is a popular cruise destination, with many itineraries including stops in Havana.

By Ferry:

Ferry services are available from other Caribbean islands, particularly the Dominican Republic.

Things to Do in Havana

  • Explore Old Havana: Wander cobblestone streets, admire colonial architecture, visit museums, and soak in the vibrant atmosphere.
  • Relax on the Malecon: Stroll along the iconic seawall, watch the sunset, and enjoy the lively atmosphere.
  • Discover Cuban Culture: Experience traditional Cuban music and dance, visit art galleries, and learn about Cuban history.
  • Enjoy Cuban Cuisine: Savor delicious Cuban food at local restaurants, paladares (family-run restaurants), or try street food vendors.
  • Take a Classic Car Ride: Experience Havana in style by taking a ride in a classic American car.
  • Visit Museums and Historical Sites: Learn about Cuba's history and culture at museums, including the Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes and the Museo de la Revolución.
  • Explore Beyond Havana: Consider day trips to nearby towns like Viñales, a picturesque valley known for its tobacco plantations and stunning scenery.

Travel Packages for Havana

There are various ways to plan your trip to Havana:

  • Group Tours: Many tour operators offer guided tours with accommodations, transportation, and sightseeing.
  • Independent Travel: Book flights, accommodations, and activities independently through online travel agencies or local providers.
  • Tailor-Made Packages: Some travel agencies offer personalized packages catering to your interests and budget.

Food and Lodging in Havana

Cuban Cuisine:

Cuban cuisine is a delicious blend of Spanish, African, and Caribbean influences. Enjoy dishes like:

  • Ropa vieja (shredded beef)
  • Lechon asado (roasted pork)
  • Arroz con pollo (chicken and rice)
  • Fresh seafood, fruits, and vegetables

Lodging Options:

Havana offers a range of accommodation options:

  • Budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses
  • Luxury hotels
  • Casas particulares (private homes offering rooms and breakfast)
